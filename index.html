about

who I am

I started in tech in 10th grade through a fellowship where I built my first app combating menstrual poverty in São Paulo, Brazil. From there I went on to doing hackathons and eventually organizing one myself at Columbia.

I enjoy reading about morphology, syntax, and semantics and how our brain is able to understand language like how you're reading the words on your screen right now or how you're able to follow what someone is saying as they speak to you which led me into the study of NLP and computational linguistics.

At Columbia, I'm currently exploring computational linguistics, systems thinking, and the intersection of language and intelligence through coursework across CS, linguistics, math, and physics.

I like to build things, learn from others, and teach what I've learned to others.
